Cholesterol Management

Cholesterol is a waxy substance found in the blood. The body needs cholesterol to build healthy cells, but excessive levels increase the risk of heart disease.

Types of Cholesterol:

LDL (Low-Density Lipoprotein)

* Known as "bad cholesterol"
* High levels increase plaque formation in arteries

HDL (High-Density Lipoprotein)

* Known as "good cholesterol"
* Helps remove excess cholesterol from the bloodstream

Triglycerides

* A type of fat in the blood
* High levels increase cardiovascular risk

Healthy Ranges:

* Total Cholesterol: Less than 200 mg/dL
* LDL: Less than 100 mg/dL
* HDL: Greater than 40 mg/dL (men)
* HDL: Greater than 50 mg/dL (women)
* Triglycerides: Less than 150 mg/dL

Ways to Lower Cholesterol:

* Reduce saturated fats
* Avoid trans fats
* Increase dietary fiber
* Exercise regularly
* Maintain a healthy weight
* Take prescribed medications when necessary

Foods That Help:

* Oats
* Beans
* Fruits
* Vegetables
* Nuts
* Fatty fish
* Olive oil
